Product Line

Modular Botanical Workstation Systems

Built for small‑scale botanical prep—rolling, sorting, trimming, and storing—without the clutter.

I’m developing workstation setups that give everything a place: tools, materials, and surfaces that actually work together. The goal is simple—keep the work area clean, keep what you need within reach, and let the layout adapt as habits and setups change.

These systems are meant to feel natural to use, not over‑engineered or complicated.

How I Build

Practical Design Backed by Real Experience

My product development is supported by access to real‑world engineering experience, including more than a decade working under IPC‑A‑610 and IPC‑7711/21 standards. That background shapes how I think about fit, repeatability, and clean, dependable design.

I use proven CAD workflows and in‑house 3D printing to move from idea to physical parts quickly, so layouts can be tested, adjusted, and refined until they feel right in actual use.

Who I Am

The Person Behind Momma’s Pop Shop

I’ve always believed in earning things the right way. I worked my way through school doing custom framing at Jo‑Ann Fabrics, then built a long‑term career in the insurance world where I became the youngest in my department to earn a CPCU. When the company needed help in accounting, I stepped in and made that role my home.

That mix of craftsmanship, financial discipline, and long‑term commitment is what I bring into this business. I run the shop with the same mindset I’ve carried my whole life: respect the details, stay organized, and build things that actually make people’s lives easier.

How That Shapes This Business

I handle the business, numbers, and quality standards. Product development is supported by access to engineering and manufacturing experience, so ideas can move from concept to real, testable hardware. Together, that combination lets me build products that are practical, reliable, and designed with real users in mind.
