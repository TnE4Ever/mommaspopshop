How We Build

Engineering‑Driven. Lean‑Minded. Built for Real Work.

Our product development is backed by more than a decade of real engineering and manufacturing experience: process engineering in electronics manufacturing, lean principles, fixture design, flow optimization, root cause analysis, sheet metal fabrication, CAD modeling, and rapid prototyping.

We think like engineers because we are engineers. If a part doesn’t reduce clutter, improve flow, or make the work easier, it doesn’t ship. We use proven CAD tools and in‑house 3D printing to move fast: build, test, break, refine, repeat. Every module in the Tabletop Soda Pop Series is shaped by this mindset.

Who I Am

The Person Behind Momma’s Pop Shop

I worked my way through school doing custom framing — learning how to measure twice, cut once, and care about the details people actually see every day.

From there, I spent years working in a not‑for‑profit insurance program that helps people who struggle to get coverage in the standard market. We do the work of a full insurance company with a lean team, which means staying organized, focused, and efficient every single day.

Along the way, I earned a CPCU (Chartered Property Casualty Underwriter) and later moved into accounting work. That mix of craftsmanship, financial discipline, and serving people who don’t always get the easy version of life is what I bring into this business. I care about details, I care about numbers, and I care about building products that make people’s day‑to‑day lives easier.

How That Shapes This Business

The business side is grounded in long‑term, not‑for‑profit work and formal training in risk and accounting. The product side is driven by more than ten years of engineering and manufacturing experience in electronics assembly, process improvement, and fabrication.

Together, that combination means we think about both sides of every decision: does it work for the person using it, and does it make sense for the business that has to build it? The result is a small company that treats quality, process, and practicality like they actually matter.
