POP Top — A Defined Work Zone For Real Benches

POP Top is the first layout in the POP Benchline System — a modular work surface designed to turn folding tables, spare rooms, and borrowed corners into defined, repeatable workstations.

It’s not a tray. It’s not a gimmick. It’s a layout: wells, grid, and working edge, all tuned for people who actually use their bench.

01 — Why This Exists
Problem First, Product Second

Because Most “Workstations” Are Either Toys Or Afterthoughts

If you’ve ever tried to do real work on a folding table, you already know the problem: everything slides, rolls, tips, and migrates. You start with a plan and end with a pile.

POP Top is a response to that — a defined work zone that gives bottles, jars, trays, and tools a place to live, so your hands and your head can stay on the actual task.

It’s not built to be the cheapest thing on the shelf. It’s built to be the thing you keep, because it actually makes your day easier.

02 — Layout Features
POP Top At A Glance

Wells, Grid, And Working Edge — Each With A Job

Bottle / Jar Wells

Defined Wells For Tall Containers

A row of circular wells sized for common bottles and jars — not just “some holes.” The goal is simple: keep tall containers from tipping, sliding, or crowding the working edge.

Container Grid

Grid Zone For Trays And Small Parts

A rectangular grid area tuned for small trays, bins, and organizers. It’s where the “stuff that moves” lives — but inside a defined boundary, not drifting across the table.

Working Edge

Front Edge For Hands‑On Work

The front band of the layout is intentionally clean — no wells, no clutter. It’s where your hands, tools, and actual work live, without fighting for space with containers.

Bench Interface

Built To Sit On Real Tables

POP Top is designed to sit on common folding tables and flat surfaces without weird overhangs or wobble. The footprint is intentional, not “whatever the CAD spit out.”

Stability

Weight, Friction, And Feel

The concept is built around enough mass and friction to stay put under normal use — not a featherweight tray that skates around every time you move your arm.

Serviceability

Built To Be Cleaned And Lived With

Surfaces, edges, and transitions are designed to be wiped down, not babied. This is meant to live in the real world, not in a product photo.

04 — Materials & Construction
Built To Be Kept, Not Replaced

Not A Disposable Accessory

The POP Top concept is built around materials that can handle real use — not the cheapest possible option that looks good in a photo and falls apart in a year.

The exact material stack will be finalized as prototypes are cut and tested, but the intent is clear: this is something you buy once, use hard, and keep.

The deeper dive on material choices, finishes, and why certain options are in or out lives on materials.html.

05 — Workflows
Real Use, Not Just Pretty Pictures

How POP Top Is Meant To Be Used

Botanical / Consumables

Controlled Chaos For Bottles And Jars

Tall containers live in the wells. Smaller jars and trays live in the grid. The front edge stays clear for actual work — measuring, mixing, sorting, or whatever your process demands.

Electronics / Small Parts

Parts Up Top, Work At The Edge

Trays of components sit in the grid zone. Tools and the active board live at the front edge. The goal is simple: you always know where things are, and nothing rolls into your solder joints.

Mixed‑Use / Shared Spaces

A Defined Zone On A Shared Table

On a folding table or shared surface, POP Top becomes “your square.” When you’re done, you can clear it off without losing the layout — the geometry stays the same every time you set it back up.
